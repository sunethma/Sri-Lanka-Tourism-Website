History

The culture of each country is distinct. Sri Lanka has a distinct culture and civilisation that has been passed down through the generations. It's also worth noting that the country has a distinct and long-standing cuisine culture. This is supported by historical literature and archaeological evidence.There is evidence that the Aryans cultivated grain before other human cultures. According to historical sources, the ancestors' meals consisted primarily of rice. Historical sources revealed some fascinating details regarding paddy agriculture. It was said in the folklore that there were more than 1,000 paddy varieties in Sri lanka.

Sri Lankans ate rice and other cereals as their primary source of nutrition. They used to have various food products such as meat, fish, eggs, and vegetables in addition to the primary stable. There were a variety of techniques for cooking rice in ancient Sri Lanka. Rice gruel, milk rice, tempered rice, ambul bath, cooked rice with bee honey, cooked rice with ghee and cooked rice with spices are some of the names for them.

However, Various food products were brought to Sri Lanka throughout the Portuguese, Dutch, and British rule periods, including carrots, beets, leaks, cabbage, mangoes, grapes, and apples.Besides, Sri Lanka has indigenous food varieties which belongs to Vedda people, some other folk groups and various areas.



What to eat?

Food from Sri Lankan culture as well as food from other cultures can be found in Sri Lanka. As a result, a visitor to this island can enjoy a variety of exquisite cuisines, including Indian, Chinese, Korean, Jain, Vietnamese, Malaysian, and Indonesian cuisines. Those diverse food cultures have a variety of main meal items, desserts, and beverages. As a result, any international visitor can eat vegetarian, vegan, or non-vegetarian meals during their trip.
